The Richwood Public Library as we know it was formed on January 26, 1915 with the organization of the first library board. However, you may not know that Richwood did in fact have an association library since 1882. On June 19, 1882, a meeting was called at the Methodist Episcopal Church to discuss the formation of a “library club.” A number of people met and the following minutes of the meeting were furnished by the secretary:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

“The Richwood Library Association met to organize. A.J. Blake was called to the chair; P.R. Mills, Secretary. Constitution presented and considered, article by article, and adopted. Rules governing the Librarian were adopted. The Association went into the election of officers, resulting as follows:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

President– A.J. Blake, Vice President– Mrs. Lizzie King, Secretary– P.R. Mills, Treasurer– G.B. Hamilton, Librarian– Robert Smith.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
